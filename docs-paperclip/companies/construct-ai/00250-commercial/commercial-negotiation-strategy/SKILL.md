# Commercial Negotiation Strategy

## Overview

Develop comprehensive negotiation strategies with counterparty analysis, BATNA assessment, concession planning, and outcome prediction to achieve favorable commercial terms.

**Announce at start:** "I'm using the commercial-negotiation-strategy skill to develop a negotiation strategy."

## When to Use This Skill

**Trigger Conditions:**
- Upcoming negotiation with counterparty
- Need to structure offer and concession strategy
- Counterparty response requires strategic adjustment
- Dispute settlement negotiation

**Prerequisites:**
- Counterparty identity and background known
- Organization's objectives and limits defined
- Market context and benchmarks available

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Counterparty Analysis

1. Identify counterparty's organizational structure
2. Assess counterparty's negotiation history
3. Identify key decision-makers and their authority
4. Analyze counterparty's financial position and pressures
5. Identify counterparty's BATNA

### Step 2: BATNA Assessment

1. Identify organization's Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement
2. Evaluate BATNA strength (strong, moderate, weak)
3. Identify counterparty's BATNA
4. Calculate Zone of Possible Agreement (ZOPA)
5. Determine walk-away points

### Step 3: Objective Setting

1. Define ideal outcome (opening position)
2. Define acceptable outcome (target position)
3. Define minimum acceptable terms (walk-away)
4. Prioritize issues (must-have, nice-to-have, tradable)

### Step 4: Concession Planning

1. List all concession options
2. Assign value to each concession (high, medium, low)
3. Plan concession sequence (smallest first, largest last)
4. Define required counterparty reciprocation for each concession
5. Set concession limits (maximum to be given)

### Step 5: Strategy Development

1. Choose approach: collaborative, competitive, accommodating, avoiding, or compromising
2. Plan opening move and initial demands
3. Develop counterparty objection responses
4. Plan dead-break strategy
5. Identify potential trade-offs and package options

### Step 6: Documentation

Generate negotiation strategy memo:
- Objectives and priorities
- BATNA analysis
- ZOPA assessment
- Concession plan
- Anticipated counterparty positions
- Key messages and talking points
- Fallback positions

## Success Criteria

- [ ] Counterparty analyzed thoroughly
- [ ] BATNA assessed for both parties
- [ ] ZOPA calculated
- [ ] Objectives prioritized (must-have, nice-to-have, tradable)
- [ ] Concession plan with sequence and limits
- [ ] Strategy documented in memo format
- [ ] Dead-break strategies planned

## Common Pitfalls

1. **No BATNA** — Negotiating without knowing your walk-away creates poor outcomes. Always define BATNA.
2. **First Concession Too Large** — Don't give too much too fast. Preserve negotiating room.
3. **Ignoring Counterparty Interests** — Understanding counterparty interests creates win-win opportunities.
4. **Emotional Decisions** — Stay focused on objectives, not emotions.
